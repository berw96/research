This project is about the effects of the Russo-Ukrainian War on both Russia and NATO's defense budgets, and gas prices throughout the European Continent. Russo-Ukrainian War

On February 24th 2022, forces from the Russian Federation invaded The Ukraine from its Eastern borders. In the months following, four oblasts (regions) - Donestk, Kherson, Luhansk and Zaporizhzhia - were formally annexed and absorbed, along with Crimea (annexed 2014), into the Russian Federation. Although, this annexation of Ukrainian territory remains unrecognized by the international community, spare North Korea.

Nord Stream 1 & 2

European dependence on Russian natural gas was facilitated by the pipelines Nord Stream 1 and Nord Stream 2, which were shut down and subsequently severely comprimised by suspected saboteurs (now assumed by Russia to have been the British Royal Navy [5]). As a consequence of gas from Russia being cut completely, gas prices in Europe have increased significantly.

Russia-NATO tensions

The North Atalantic Trade Organisation or NATO was founded in the light of WW2 in April of 1949 as an oppositional alliance between the Western nations - of democratic, capitalist orientation - and the Eastern nations (including Russia) of authortarian, socialist/communist orientation. The Soviet Union had annexed all of Eastern Europe, including East Germany (then the German Democratic Republic), and it was NATO that was formed to monitor and decelerate any attempts by the Soviets to further expand their influence to the West. The equivalent to NATO for the Eastern bloc was the Warsaw Pact, formed by the USSR, Poland, Bulgaria, Hungary, Romania, Albania, East Germany, and Czechoslovakia.

Throughout the Cold War (1947-1991) and up until today, tensions have remained consistent between the two ideologies, and for nearly a decade since 2014 Crimean War these tensions have been particularly present between Russia and NATO (with the USA spearheading the organisation's stance in the world). The tensions are partly geopolitical, stemming from the presence of NATO in the EU and former USSR states (Ukraine, Belarus, Latvia, Estonia, etc), and Russia's assaults on Ukraine. It is these tensions which serve as ground to explore correlation between the two side's military budgets, and the energy prices in Europe which have risen significantly due to Russian sanctions.

Russia is largely invulnerable as far as invasion goes - it is surrounded by artic sheilds to the North and East, and both mountainous ranges and allies to the South, making the act of ground invasion largely impractical. However, there exists a small corridor to its West which leads all the way into the capital Moscow, known as the East European Plain. Russian incentive to annex or gain significant influence in East Europe centers around this geography - they want buffer states to prevent or hinder invasion from the West. Hence, the attempts of Ukraine - which lies on Russia's doorstep - to edge closer to the influence of the West (European Union and NATO) stirs concern in Russia. The invasion of Ukraine is partly justified by Russian officials due to the history which Ukraine shares with Russia, with the latter claiming the former as a long lost sibling.

Methods

Exploratory Data Analysis

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) involves the high-level summary of data. This includes the following:

  • creating visualizations for the data sets
  • summarising key aspects (e.g., central tendencies, standard deviation)
  • drawing relationships between observations and response variables
  • establishing a basis for deeper analysis

Visualization, central tendency (e.g., arithmetic mean, median, mode), and standard deviation are crucial to EDA as they effectively and succinctly communicate data, in one image or value. Visualization enables us to observe trends and distributions in data sets. Central tendency provides insight to the value(s) which are representative of our data. Standard deviation signals to us how much stochastic observations deviate from an established central tendency (specifically the arithmetic mean).

Standard Deviation

The standard deviation ($\sigma$) of a data set measures the dispertion of values relative to its arithmetic mean. Equal to the square root of variance, the standard deviation of a set of values is given by:

$$\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum_{i=1}^{i=n}(x_{i}-\mu)^{2}}{n}}$$

where $\mu$ is the population mean of the data set, which contains $n$ observed values.

Correlation

The correlation between two stochastic variables measures how they affect each other. Correlation may be non-existent ($0$), positive ($+ve$), such that the two variables trend with a positive gradient, or negative ($-ve$), such that the two variables trend with a negative gradient. The correlation is seldom perfect (magnitude 1), and in instances where it is perfect, suspicion should be raised as to the viability of the data used.

For continuous, regressional data, it is required that said data be normalized before any correlations can be drawn. Normalized data adheres to a frequency distribution of values close to its second quartile (central tendency), and exhibits little to no skewness.

For linear models, we use the Pearson Correlation Coefficient, given by the following expression:

$$r_{xy} = \frac{n\sum_{i=1}^{i=n}x_{i}y_{i} - \sum_{i=1}^{i=n}x_{i}\sum_{i=1}^{i=n}y_{i}}{\sqrt{n\sum_{i=1}^{i=n}x_{i}^{2} - (\sum_{i=1}^{i=n}x_{i})^{2}}\sqrt{n\sum_{i=1}^{i=n}y_{i}^{2}-(\sum_{i=1}^{i=n}y_{i})^{2}}}$$

which measures the correlation (mutual effect) between a given input $x_{i}$ and its corresponding output $y_{i}$, for $n$ observations in the provided data set.

Skewness

Skewness measures the imbalance of a dataset from being normally distributed. A Gaussian distribution exhibiting a (near) perfect bell curve when plotted would possess zero skewness, for instance. Skewness may be either positive ($+ve$) wherein the tail of the dataset is longer on the right, or negative ($-ve$) wherein it is longer on the left.

Galton Skewness (AKA Bowley's Skewness) considers the upper and lower quartiles ($Q_1$ and $Q_3$) and the median ($Q_2$) of the dataset:

$$GS := \frac{Q_1 + Q_3 - 2Q_2}{Q_3 - Q_1}$$

Python Libraries

The Pandas and Numpy libraries are used throughout the analysis involved in this project. Pandas provides convenient means of reading data from a CSV (Comma Separated Value) file into an entity known as a data frame, which serves to organize the data in a tabular format, and it additionally provides functions suitable for EDA (e.g., plotting, correlation, standard deviation, central tendencies). Numpy is utilized for calculating the arithmetic mean of data columns, which is useful for succinctly describing a range of observations with one value.

Discussion

Since 2014, Russia and NATO have exhibited contrasting trends towards military spending. Where NATO has been increasing, Russia has been decreasing steadily. This may be associated with the regime changes of the United States - NATO's single greatest investor. Since 2014, three different American presidents have taken office - Barack Obama, Donald J. Trump, and Joe Biden and each governed under a different mantra. It was the Trump administration, though, who increased American spending towards NATO defense, and the Biden administration who has maintained this trend.

NATO defense budget shares a strong positive correlation with the yearly average gas prices of Europe. This arises from the sociopolitical relationship between the two quantities, as a result of tensions with Russia, who until September 2022 was the chief supplier of gas to Europe.

Hypothesis

Due to the war-centric relationship between NATO and Russia...

decisions by NATO to increase spending have economic repercussions for European members as a direct result of Russian tactics.

The economic, environmental, and health related effects of hightened cost in natural gas in Europe may be further explored with other factors taken into consideration, such as

  • the cost of living
  • renewable energy usage
  • carbon emissions
  • health issues or deaths due to cold temperatures

Although, war has sociological effects, too. The hypothesis that NATO's military budget directly affects Europe's economy (due to her historic dependence on Russian exports) has social parameters which can be investigated under the foundation of these findings:

  • anti-Russian sentiment
  • anti-Ukrainian sentiment
  • anti-West sentiment
  • refugees from Eastern Europe

For example: European citizens feeling the effects of the war, be they economic or personal, would exhibit political alignment which influences their sentiment. Some people may agree or disagree with the war in Ukraine. Some people may find themselves being highly critical of Russian individuals (Russophobia), even those who are not directly involved with the conflict.

Summary

Using high-level exploratory data analysis, and data downloaded from online sources [1-3], a relationship between defense spending and gas prices from 2017 and 2022 was uncovered. The data used was organized manually into Excel spreadsheets, exported as CSV and then interpreted as data frames with Pandas for analysis.

With the strong correlation between NATO budget and gas prices in Europe established, the invitation for deeper analysis on their implications is clear. The next question to answer is: does anti-Russian sentiment correspond to the effects of increased gas prices?
